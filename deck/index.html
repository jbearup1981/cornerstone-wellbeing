In Plain English

What this program is.

A pre-tax payroll deduction wraps a fixed-indemnity wellbeing benefit for the employee. The employee sees a larger net paycheck and a monthly wellbeing benefit of roughly $1,200. The employer pays a small admin fee, funded by FICA savings on the same deduction.

No new HR work for the employer. No deductible to chase. No benefit to design. The infrastructure is already built — this is distribution and operations on top of it.

Mechanics, at a glance

  • Section 125 pre-tax deduction from the employee's check
  • Carrier issues a scheduled wellbeing benefit (~$1,200/mo)
  • Indemnity payments coded as reimbursement, not wages
  • Employer admin fee funded by FICA savings
  • Bundled virtual primary care + Rx via Amaze Health

Case Routing

Two buckets. Economics follow the work.

Nexus is not trying to eat Capitol's existing book. Deals outside the Nexus Standard profile route back to Capitol on reduced Nexus comp — distribution partners still get paid, the client still gets placed, and Nexus stays out of the day-to-day.

Bucket 1

Nexus Standard — end-to-end

  • 50–1,000 EE (sweet spot 50–300)
  • Stable workforce, low turnover
  • Wage bands that genuinely qualify
  • Real payroll function with a competent owner
  • Operational maturity — HR and finance engaged

Full Nexus economics. Nexus does the work; Nexus is compensated for it.

Bucket 2

Capitol-Routed — reduced Nexus comp

  • Staffing companies
  • Farming, seasonal, restaurants, food service
  • High-turnover industries generally
  • Groups requiring API-level payroll integration
  • Groups materially larger than the sweet spot

Capitol owns the operational lift through its existing broker and TPA network. Nexus contributes distribution only.

Said plainly: the less work Nexus does, the less Nexus earns. That keeps the incentive aligned and removes any reason to compete for cases Capitol's existing brokers are already serving well.

The Eligibility Loop

  1. Nexus system produces and maintains the eligibility file per employer
  2. CEHAS ingests the file weekly
  3. CEHAS generates invoice → ACH from employer

Required Fields

Employer/employee ID, plan election, enrollment status, opt-out flag, non-qualified flag, employee email, dependents, coverage effective date, term date.

Open Decision
